Feeding Guidelines

Learn how to properly feed and nourish stray cats to keep them healthy and strong.

Nutritional Requirements

Stray cats need a balanced diet to maintain their health and energy levels. Here are the key nutritional requirements:

  • High-quality protein from meat sources
  • Essential fatty acids for coat health
  • Taurine for heart and eye health
  • Clean, fresh water available at all times

Feeding Schedule

Establishing a consistent feeding schedule helps cats know when to expect food and prevents waste:

  • Feed at the same times each day
  • Morning and evening feedings are ideal
  • Remove uneaten wet food after 30 minutes
  • Keep dry food protected from weather

Food Safety

Proper food handling and storage is crucial for the health of stray cats:

  • Store food in airtight containers
  • Check expiration dates regularly
  • Clean feeding areas daily
  • Use elevated feeding stations when possible

Best Practices

Follow these guidelines to ensure safe and effective feeding:

  • Choose quiet, sheltered feeding locations
  • Space feeding stations to prevent territorial conflicts
  • Monitor food consumption to identify health issues
  • Keep feeding areas clean and sanitary
