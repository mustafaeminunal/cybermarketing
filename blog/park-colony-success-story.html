The Park Colony

A community coming together to save eight precious lives

The Park Colony: A Community Success Story

March 10, 2024 Success Story

In the heart of Brașov, a colony of eight cats had made their home in a local park. What started as a challenging rescue mission turned into one of our most successful community projects, demonstrating the power of collaboration and compassion.

Discovery and Initial Assessment

Local residents first alerted us to the colony in November 2023. The cats had been living in the park for several years, surviving on occasional handouts from park visitors. The colony included cats of various ages, from young adults to seniors, all in need of medical attention and proper care.

Community Engagement

What made this rescue special was the incredible support from the local community. Residents helped us identify feeding patterns, regular hangout spots, and even assisted in setting up feeding stations. Local businesses contributed supplies and resources to support our rescue efforts.

The Rescue Operation

Over six weeks, our team worked methodically to gain the trust of each cat. Using humane traps and regular feeding schedules, we were able to rescue all eight cats safely. Each cat received full veterinary care, including vaccinations, deworming, and spaying/neutering.

The Colony Members

  • Whiskers - Senior male tabby, now living with a retired couple
  • Shadow & Luna - Bonded pair, adopted together by a young family
  • Tiger - Adult orange tabby, found home with a local teacher
  • Mittens & Socks - Sister cats, adopted by neighboring families
  • Pepper - Young black cat, now thriving with a first-time cat owner
  • Storm & Cloud - Brother cats, living happily with a cat-loving couple

Project Achievements:

  • 8 cats rescued and rehomed
  • 6 weeks of coordinated rescue efforts
  • 15+ community volunteers involved
  • 100% adoption success rate

Impact on the Community

This success story did more than just help eight cats - it brought the community together and raised awareness about responsible pet ownership. The park is now monitored regularly by our volunteers to prevent future colonies from forming, and we've established a network of local supporters who help us identify and assist other cats in need.
